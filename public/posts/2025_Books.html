Book Recommendations - 2025 Edition

1. For Fans of Fiction, Magic, and Mystery

The Broken Earth Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin If you love immersive worlds and intricate storytelling, this trilogy is a must-read. The plot unfolds at a steady pace, keeping you curious and engaged. Jemisin’s creativity and ability to weave complex narratives are truly impressive, and the main character’s development is deeply satisfying.

A glimpse into the story: The setting is a planet with a single supercontinent called Stillness. Every few centuries, its inhabitants face the “Fifth Season,” a catastrophic climate event. Society is divided into various communities, ethnicities, and species, all striving for harmony. Yet, fear and prejudice linger—especially toward the Orogenes, individuals with extraordinary abilities.

2. For Fans of Science and Psychology

Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman This classic explores how our minds work by breaking down two modes of thought:

  • System 1: Fast, instinctive and emotional
  • System 2: Slow, deliberate and logical
Kahneman explains what triggers each system and how they interact, offering fascinating insights into decision-making and human behavior.

3. For Mystery and Crime Enthusiasts

The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides Even though I’m not usually a fan of thrillers, this book surprised me. I read it over the summer and couldn’t put it down. The plot twist is brilliant, and the storytelling is sharp and engaging. It’s an easy yet gripping read—perfect for a weekend escape.

4. For Lovers of Heartwarming Novels

Before the Coffee Gets Cold by Toshikazu Kawaguchi Set in a small Tokyo café, this novel offers a magical premise: the chance to travel back in time—if you follow a few strict rules. The book follows four customers, each with a unique story. It’s a cozy, reflective read, ideal for quiet winter nights.
